Bill Belichick

Bill Belichick has been in the league for 45 years after his father Steve Belichick introduced him to the game while coaching the United States Naval Academy. He would start from the bottom working for his mentor, Bill Parcells, after named Defensive Coordinator, winning 2 titles against the Denver Broncos and Buffalo Bills. Belichick would soon start a head coaching job with the Cleveland Browns having little success with just one playoff win which was against his former mentor (Would be Cleveland's last playoff win till this day). He would go to New England and New York to join his former mentor with a trip to Super Bowl XXXI, Bill would later resign from the Head Coaching job a day after Parcells retired as a head coach of the New York Jets until he would return to New England with the Head Coaching job, leading them to 9 super bowl appearances with 6 of them ending with a win.

2019 SEASON

The 2019 season has been an impressive year for the defense and special teams. However, Brady and the offense has yet to take themselves in an explosive level after struggling against the Bills, Ravens, Eagles, Texans and Chiefs with their defense stopping this Patriot's Offense.This frustration has led to some questions whether or not if the patriots can even make a deep playoff run with this offense relying on a veteran slot receiver in Jules, Mohamed Sanu, a couple of young wideouts and even a bottom ranked group of Tight Ends. Although these pass catchers have some talent, they can't seem to get the trust of Brady, taking hit after hit afer hit with the amount of time he has been in the pocket.

Super Bowl XX was an American football game played at Mercedes-Benz Superdome, New Orleans, on January 26, 1986, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 1985 season. With the National Football Conference champion Chicago Bears defeating the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots, 10-46.

Super Bowl XXXI was an American football game played at Mercedes-Benz Superdome, New Orleans, on January 26, 1997, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 1996 season. With the National Football Conference champion Green Bay Packers defeating the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots, 21-35.

Super Bowl XXXVI was an American football game played at Mercedes-Benz Superdome, New Orleans, on February 3, 2002, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2001 season. With the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots defeating the National Football Conference champion St. Louis Rams, 20-17.

Super Bowl XXXVIII was an American football game played at NRG Stadium in Houston, Texas, on February 1, 2004, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2003 season. With the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots defeating the National Football Conference champion Carolina Panthers, 32-29.

Super Bowl XXXIX was an American football game played at TIAA Bank Field Stadium, Jacksonville, Florida, on February 6, 2005, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2004 season. With the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots defeating the National Football Conference champion Philadelphia Eagles, 24-21.

Super Bowl XLII was an American football game played at University of Phoenix Stadium, Glendale, Arizona, on February 3, 2008, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2007 season. With the National Football Conference champion New York Giants defeating the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots, 14-17.

Super Bowl XLVI was an American football game played at Lucas Oil Stadium, Indianapolis, Indiana, on February 5, 2012, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2011 season. With the National Football Conference champion New York Giants defeating the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots, 17-21.

Super Bowl XLIX was an American football game played at University of Phoenix Stadium, Glendale, Arizona, on February 1, 2015, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2014 season. With the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots defeating the National Football Conference champion Seattle Seahawks, 28-24.

Super Bowl LI was an American football game played at NRG Stadium in Houston, Texas, on February 5, 2017, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2016 season. With the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots defeating the National Football Conference champion Atlanta Falcons from a 28-3 deficit, 34-28.

Super Bowl LII was an American football game played at U.S. Bank Stadium, Minneapolis, Minnesota, on February 4, 2018, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2017 season. With the National Football Conference champion Philadelphia Eagles defeating the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots, 33-41.

Super Bowl LIII was an American football game played at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, Georgia, on February 3, 2019, to determine the champion of the National Football League for the 2018 season. With the American Football Conference champion New England Patriots defeating the National Football Conference champion Los Angeles Rams, 13–3.
