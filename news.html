Monday, Sep 09, 2019

Patriots sign WR Antonio Brown

BREAKING NEWS - The New England Patriots announced that they have signed former Oakland Raiders WR Antonio Brown. Brown, a veteran of nine seasons with Pittsburgh from 2010-2018 has played in 130 games with 103 starts and totaled 837 receptions for 11,207 yards and 74 touchdown receptions. This is a signing of a one year 15 million dollar deal with 9 million in signing bonuses.


Friday, Sep 20, 2019 (Updated)

Patriots release WR Antonio Brown

BREAKING NEWS - The New England Patriots announced that they will depart ways with WR Antonio Brown. Brown, who the receiver was accused by a former trainer, Britney Taylor, of rape and sexual assault in a civil lawsuit filed on Sept 10 with the league currently investigating these allegations leaving the Patriots short on weapons.

Patriots trade 2nd-rounder for Mohamed Sanu

The New England Patriots went straight to a trade after defdeating the New York Jets early morning by trading a 2nd round draft pick for Falcons WR Mohamed Sanu. Mohamed Sanu can be both a slot receiver and a red zone target with good height, being able to boost an offense that has started to show signs of struggles with competitive teams ahead in their schedule.

Tuesday, Dec 31, 2019

Pariots have gave permission for teams to interview Josh McDaniels

The New England Patriots have allowed teams such as the New York Giants, the Cleveland Browns and the Carolina Panthers to interview Josh McDaniels for a job as their Head Coach along with Patriot's special teams coordinator and wide receivers coach, Joe Judge. McDaniels wasn't aware of teams interest to interview him as recently as Monday. Coaching Canidates who are busy in wildcard weekend are not permitted to be interviewed this week and with McDaniels focused on the game rather than the interviews. “When that stuff is presented to me or discussed with me or whatever, then we’ll deal with it appropriately,’’ he said Monday. “Honestly, it’s pretty easy for me in terms of formula. I’m the offensive coordinator of the Patriots. We have a lot of work to do, we can improve in a lot of areas to try to get ready for this week — our biggest game of the year against, certainly, the best team we’ve played since a postseason game. Our players deserve that, our coaches deserve that, and that’s what they’re going to get from me.’’

Thursday, Jan 2, 2020

Rumors - Could This Be Tom Brady's Last Game With The Patriots?

Patriots QB, Tom Brady, is on his way to play in wildcard weekend in which the team hasn't been for a decade, being gifted with the bye week for the past 10 years. However, his game is showing signs of mortality after struggling to keep pace with the team's young WR's (rookies) and his inaccurate passes when they needed the ball to open receievers the most. Brady said he wants to play to 45, leaving three more seasons to complete to achieve such a goal. It's possible but it just might not happen in New England. With the team getting ready to face the fearless Titans Saturday at 8:15pm EST, will it be Brady's last game in New England or will he stay to fufill his goal in New England?
